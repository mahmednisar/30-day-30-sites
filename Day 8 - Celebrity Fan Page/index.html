Ada Lovelace

The First Computer Programmer

A portrait of Ada Lovelace with a diagram from her notes behind her. The diagram shows the variables to be used by Babbage's Difference Engine.

Timeline of Lovelace's life:

  • 1815 - Born Augusta Ada Byron in London, England to Lady Wentworth and Lord Byron.
  • 1816 - Her parents separate. Lord Byron leaves the country and dies eight years later, never having returned.
  • 1832 - Her mathematical abilities and interest in the subject, including differential calculus, begin to become apparent.
  • 1833 - Meets Charles Babbage, through Ada's private tutor Mary Somerville.
  • 1835 - Marries William King, becoming Lady King.
  • 1838 - Becomes Countess of Lovelace.
  • 1842 - 1843 - Over a nine month period Lovelace translates an article on Babbage's Difference engine written by Luigi Menabrea, an Italian engineer. Her notes on the article include what is now considered to be the first computer program.
  • 1844
    • Expresses an interest in creating a mathematical model to describe the workings of the human brain, although this is never completed.
    • Learns to experiment with electricity from engineer Charles Cross.
    • Reviews an article called Researches in Magnetism that is never published.
  • 1852 - Dies of uterine cancer, age 36.
  • 1953 - Lovelace's notes on the Analytical Engine are republished.
